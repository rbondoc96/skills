# Email

Apply the voice from `SKILL.md`. Email is the most formal channel, but formal means complete and clear, not padded. Stay concise. No em-dashes.

## Shape

- **Subject:** specific and scannable. The reader should know what it's about and whether it needs action. "Staging deploy blocked, need migration approval" beats "Quick question".
- **Open with the point.** First sentence states why you're writing or what you need. No "I hope you're well" windup.
- **Body:** the minimum context needed, then the ask or the info. Bullets for anything with multiple parts.
- **Close with the next step.** What happens next, who does it, by when.

## Register by recipient

- **Teammates** → light. A greeting and a sign-off, casual and short.
- **Cross-team / unfamiliar** → complete sentences, a bit more context, still tight.
- **Clients / external** → most formal: polished, no slang, less jargon. Courteous but not obsequious. Still lead with the point.

## Greetings & sign-offs

- Greeting: `Hi <name>,` by default. Match the thread if one exists.
- Sign-off: `Warm regards,` followed by `<user_first_name>`.
- Don't over-apologize or over-thank. One "thanks" is enough.

## Examples

To a teammate:
```
Subject: Staging deploy blocked, need migration approval

Hi Sam,

The staging deploy is blocked on the DB migration. Can you approve it when you get a sec? I need it to test the webhook fix before EOD.

Warm regards,
<user_first_name>
```

To a client (more formal, same directness):
```
Subject: Timeline update: reporting module

Hi Alex,

Quick update on the reporting module: the core work is done and in review. We're on track to ship by Friday.

One open item: we need the finalized export format from your team to complete the last piece. Could you send that over by Wednesday?

Warm regards,
<user_first_name>
```
